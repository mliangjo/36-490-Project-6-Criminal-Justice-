Legal Context - Florida

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - Infractions. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - Non-criminal violations. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - 2nd degree misdemeanors. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - 1st degree misdemeanors. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - Arrestee poses a danger to self or others. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule
        3.125(b)]
      - There is reasonable suspicion that the arrestee will fail to appear in
        court. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - Arrestee has previously failed to respond to a notice to appear. [Fla.
        R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - Arrestee is believed to be wanted in another jurisdiction. [Fla. R.
        Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - Arrestee refuses to sign the notice to appear. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule
        3.125(b)]
      - Arrestee cannot be identified or refuses to identify him or herself.
        [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - The arresting officer. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(b)]
      - The booking officer. [Fla. R. Crim. P. Rule 3.125(c)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana is illegal. [Fla. Stat. § 893.13(6)(b) (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petitioner obtains a certificate of eligibility. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petitioner has never had a criminal record expunged. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petition or request is for a single arrest or a single incident of
        alleged criminal activity.  An individual may have multiple arrests
        expunged only if the arrests directly relate to the same offense or
        incident.. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The relevant criminal charge is not for certain sex offenses and does
        not require registration as a sexual predator. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585
        (2013)]
      - The petitioner has never been adjudicated guilty of a comparable offense
        or violation, and has never been adjudicated delinquent for committing
        certain violent offenses. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petitioner is no longer under court supervision that resulted from
        the relevant arrest or alleged criminal activity. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585
        (2013)]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures. [Fla.
        Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The prosecutor has made a final determination that no charges will be
        filed. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were not prosecuted. [Fla. Stat.
        § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were dismissed. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013)]
      - The charges where expunction is sought did not result in a trial. [Fla.
        Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [Fla. Stat. § 985.556(2) (2013)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [Fla. Stat. § 985.03(7) (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Fla. Stat. §

      - The petitioner obtains a certificate of eligibility. [Fla. Stat. §

        943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petition or request is for a single arrest or a single incident of
        alleged criminal activity.  An individual may have multiple arrests
        expunged only if the arrests directly relate to the same offense or
        incident.. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]

        not require registration as a sexual predator. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585
        (2013)]

        or violation, and has never been adjudicated delinquent for committing
        certain violent offenses. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]

        the relevant arrest or alleged criminal activity. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585
        (2013)]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures. [Fla.
        Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]
      - The prosecutor has made a final determination that no charges will be
        filed. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]

        § 943.0585 (2013)]

        Stat. § 943.0585 (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013),Florida Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The petitioner obtains a certificate of eligibility. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013),Florida Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The petitioner has never had a criminal record expunged. [Fla. Stat. §
        943.0585 (2013),Florida Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The relevant criminal charge is not for certain sex offenses and does
        not require registration as a sexual predator. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585
        (2013),Florida Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The petitioner has never been adjudicated guilty of a comparable offense
        or violation, and has never been adjudicated delinquent for committing
        certain violent offenses. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013),Florida
        Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013),Florida
        Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The petitioner is no longer under court supervision that resulted from
        the relevant arrest or alleged criminal activity. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585
        (2013),Florida Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The charges to which the expunction request pertains did not result in a
        trial. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013),Florida Department of Law
        Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The court withheld an adjudication of guilt for the charges for which
        expunction is sought. [Fla. Stat. § 943.0585 (2013),Florida Department
        of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8), (9)]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures. [Fla.
        Stat. § 943.0585 (2013),Florida Department of Law Enforcement F.A.Q (8),
        (9)]

        940.01(2)  (2013),Fla. Stat. § 940.03 (2013)]

        (2013)]
      - Approval from the state legislature if the offense to be pardoned is an

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Judges. [Fla. Stat. § 397.334(2) (2013)]
      - Prosecutors. [Fla. Stat. § 832.08(4) (2013)]
      - Defendants. [Fla. Stat. § 397.334(2) (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to refer and admit candidates to diversion
        programs. [Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Probation without verdict. [Fla. Stat. § 948.08 (2013)]
      - Community service, control, or supervision programs. [Fla. Stat. §
        948.08 (2013)]
      - Vocational programs.
      - Substance abuse treatment programs. [Fla. Stat. § 397.334(1), (5)
        (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.16 (2013)]
      - Veterans programs. [Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.16
        (2013)]
      - Bad check programs. [Fla. Stat. § 832.08 (2013)]
      - Education programs. [Fla. Stat. § 948.08 (2013)]
      - Mental health court. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16 (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to implement any pretrial diversion program,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [Fla. Stat. § 397.334(1), (5)
        (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.08 (2013)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants with no prior convictions.
      - Defendants with no prior felony convictions. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16(1)(a)
        (2013)]
      - Veterans who suffer from a military-related mental illness, a traumatic
        brain injury, or a substance abuse problem. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16(2)(a)
        (2013),Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013)]
      - Defendants identified as controlled substance abusers. [Fla. Stat. §
        948.08(6)(a) (2013)]
      - Defendants identified as alcohol abusers. [Fla. Stat. § 948.08(6)(a)
        (2013)]
      - Defendants not currently serving a probation sentence.
      - Defendants not currently participating in a diversion program.
      - Defendants who voluntarily agree to participation. [Fla. Stat. §
        948.16(1)(a) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.08(2) (2013),Fla. Stat. §
        948.08(6)(a) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.08(8)(a) (2013)]
      - Defendants identified as having a mental illness. [Fla. Stat. §
        948.16(3) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.08(8)(a) (2013)]
      - Defendants not previously convicted of a serious violent offense.
      - Defendants not previously convicted of more than one nonviolent
        misdemeanor. [Fla. Stat. § 948.08(2) (2013)]
      - Defendants living in a county with a population under 100,000.
      - Defendants not previously convicted of certain possession offenses.
      - Counties are authorized to determine the eligibility of defendants,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [Fla. Stat. § 832.08(1) (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - All misdemeanors. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16(1)(a), (2)(a), (3)  (2013),Fla.
        Stat. § 948.08(2), (6)(a), (7)(a) (2013)]
      - Low-level felonies. [Fla. Stat. § 948.08(2), (6)(a), (7)(a) (2013)]
      - Nonviolent felonies. [Fla. Stat. § 948.08(2), (6)(a), (7)(a) (2013)]
      - Certain offenses related to prostitution. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16(1)(a),
        (2)(a), (3)  (2013),Fla. Stat. § 948.08(2), (6)(a), (7)(a) (2013)]
      - Certain offenses related to the use or possession of drugs and
        paraphernalia. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16(1)(a), (2)(a), (3)  (2013),Fla.
        Stat. § 948.08(2), (6)(a), (7)(a) (2013)]
      - Underage possession of alcohol. [Fla. Stat. § 948.16(1)(a), (2)(a), (3) 
        (2013)]
      - Certain offenses related to issuing worthless checks or ceasing to make
        payments with the intention to defraud. [Fla. Stat. § 832.08(1) (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [Fla. Stat. §
        832.08(1) (2013)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

        948.08(6)(a) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013)]

        948.08(6)(a) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013)]

        948.08(6)(a) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013)]

        948.08(6)(a) (2013),Fla. Stat. § 394.47891 (2013)]
      - Burglary or attempted burglary when it constitutes a 1st or 2nd degree
